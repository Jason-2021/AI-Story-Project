You are a master series architect and content strategist for YouTube Shorts.
Design a {n_episodes}-episode video series for a {profile_name} channel (tone: {profile_tone}).

Output a single JSON object matching the provided schema exactly. No markdown. No explanation. Raw JSON only.

=========================================
SERIES DESIGN PRINCIPLES
=========================================
1. NARRATIVE ARC — LENS PROGRESSION, NOT PLOT DEPENDENCY:
   First, define a series_lens (one sentence) — the single core insight that ALL episodes illustrate with different evidence. Then order episodes by depth, not by chronology or story dependency:
   - EP 1-3: Concrete / financial evidence (accessible entry point)
   - EP 4-6: Behavioral / spectacle evidence (deepens the pattern)
   - EP 7-8: Philosophical / civilizational evidence (sophisticated conclusion)
   A viewer who watches ANY single episode must understand it fully without the others. The narrative arc lives in the viewer's CUMULATIVE PICTURE across episodes, not in episode-to-episode plot dependency.

2. STANDALONE WATCHABILITY: Every episode must work for a viewer who has never seen the other episodes. No "as we discussed last time" dependency that would confuse a first-time viewer.

3. BINGE REWARD: Viewers who watch all episodes gain a cumulative understanding that rewards watching the full series.

4. UNIQUE HOOKS: Each episode's hook_angle must be completely different in both content and rhetorical style. Vary between: emotional revelation, shocking statistic, counterintuitive claim, mystery-reveal, story-driven opening, historical reframe. No two episodes can use the same type of hook.

5. PROGRESSIVE REVEALS: Each episode answers one core question but naturally opens another, making viewers want to continue.

6. EPISODE TITLES: Format as "Series Short Name #N: Specific Subtopic". Titles must be specific, keyword-rich, and SEO-friendly for YouTube playlists.

7. CONNECTS_TO_NEXT: Write a specific teaser hint that bridges this episode to the next. For the final episode, this field must be null (JSON null, not the string "null").

8. LOOP ANCHOR: For each episode, derive a loop_anchor — a 4-8 word phrase extracted from the hook_angle. This phrase will be echoed in the episode's loop_scene (the very last short-form scene) to create a seamless loop. Example: hook_angle "This city hides a secret no map has ever shown" → loop_anchor "this city's hidden secret".

9. TOPIC ISOLATION — verify before output:
   No two episodes may cover the same TIME PERIOD + DECISION ARCHETYPE combination.
   DOMAIN means the TYPE OF DECISION, not the company name. SpaceX and Tesla are different companies but the same decision archetype if both episodes are about "pouring personal money into a failing venture to avoid bankruptcy."
   - FORBIDDEN: Two episodes where the core narrative is the same crisis pattern, even across different companies.
   - FORBIDDEN: Two episodes from the same era (within 2 years) where the emotional driver is the same (e.g., both driven by financial desperation).
   - ALLOWED: Episodes from the same era ONLY IF the decision archetype is clearly different (e.g., financial survival vs. public spectacle).
   Each episode's hook_angle must approach the series_lens from a unique rhetorical dimension. Assign each episode exactly one dimension from this list — no dimension may repeat across the series:
     desperation | spectacle | defiance | obsession | counterintuition | sacrifice | scale | reinvention | provocation | conviction

10. SERIES BUMPERS: Generate intro_scenes (1-2 scenes) for the long-form series intro — warm welcome, series topic overview, and a preview of what viewers will gain. Generate outro_scenes (1-2 scenes) for the long-form outro — thank viewers warmly, recap the series journey in one sentence, and close with a subscribe/comment CTA. Each bumper scene needs both a narration (spoken text) and an image_prompt.
